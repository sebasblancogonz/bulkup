# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

People who already train and already have a plan — a routine and/or diet a coach handed them as a PDF or a photo. Lifters and gym-goers mid-program who care about progressing: sets, weights, PRs, body composition, adherence. They've tried other fitness apps and can smell a generic template or a faked metric from a mile away. Context: on their phone (and Apple Watch) at the gym or between sets, not at a desk. The job to be done: stop letting their plan rot as a dead PDF in Downloads, and actually log and *see* whether they're progressing — answer the one question that nags them, "is this working?".

## Product Purpose

BulkUp turns the training/diet plan you already have (PDF or photo) into a fully trackable iOS + Apple Watch app: log every set, weight and PR, follow your diet, track body composition, keep streaks, compete with friends. AI parses the plan once so you never copy it by hand again. The product is pre-launch — the website's single job is waitlist capture (founder access). Success = qualified, real signups who feel they're joining an athletic brand, not a SaaS waitlist.

## Brand Personality

The training partner who's been lifting eight years, not the influencer. Direct to the point of dry, but on your side. Speaks in kilos, sets, weeks, streaks and maxes — data is the argument, not emotion as filler. Tutea (informal "you"), short sentences, action verbs, zero hype. Three words: **directo, disciplinado, sin humo**. Archetype: a disciplined **Hero** (proof through repeated effort) with a **Sage** undertone (the formulas, the numbers, 1,000+ exercises — the Sage serves the Hero, never leads). The brand's enemy is **invisible progress**: training hard with no idea if you're advancing. Canonical copy is **Spanish-first**, mirrored to English in the same voice. Hard bans: clichés ("transforma tu vida", "siguiente nivel", "potenciar"), SaaS lexicon ("medible/personalizada" as filler, "configuración manual"), self-applied "premium", and "con IA" in a headline.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS / tech startup** — corporate language, the hero-metric template, an eyebrow above every section, cold and templated.
- **Soft consumer app (Foodnoms-light)** — white background, rounded display type, oversized universal radius, flat/shadowless. Exactly the system this brand just left.
- **Loud supplement bro-marketing** — screaming neon, exaggerated hype, poster-cliché motivation.
- **Cold corporate dashboard** — tables, soulless KPIs, gray on gray.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the body, not just the screen.** Earn the athletic feeling with real effort/gym imagery before showing UI; pair raw photo + crisp UI in the same frame. Screenshots alone are half the message.
2. **The athlete is the hero, the PDF is not.** Lead with the outcome (visible progress); demote the AI/PDF mechanism to a friction-killer in the subtext, never the headline.
3. **Data as proof, honestly.** Make the numbers (1RM, %, streaks) shine in mono. Never fake a metric — no invented counters; real scarcity ("founder spots") beats a fake herd.
4. **Lima is a blade, not a brush.** The lime accent earns ≤10% of the surface; everything else graphite + bone-white. Premium is shown, never announced.
5. **Earn the word, cut the rest.** Copy sounds like someone who trains: every line specific, no SaaS filler, no empty motivation.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG **AA** over the graphite theme — body/muted text ≥4.5:1, large/UI ≥3:1 (muted `#9BA39E` on bg `#0E1110` ≈ 7:1). Native `<details>` semantics for the FAQ; visible lime focus rings; `prefers-reduced-motion` honored globally and per-section; meaningful, bilingual alt text. Reveal animations enhance an already-visible default — content is never gated on a JS class, so it ships visible in headless/no-JS renders.
